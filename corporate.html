Design Elements

Please provide a list of design elements alongside their justifications:

  • A colour scheme

    • #541C1C Rich Maroon: This deep, warm hue exudes a sense of sophistication and stability, instilling confidence and trust in the brand. The rich maroon also creates a feeling of warmth and comfort, reflecting the nostalgic and homely essence of Homecrate's service.

    • #9F5B31 Warm Brown: This earthy, inviting color evokes feelings of familiarity and reliability, resonating with the brand's commitment to connecting expats with their roots. The warm brown also embodies the natural and authentic elements of the carefully curated packages, highlighting Homecrate's dedication to delivering genuine, heartfelt experiences.

    • #E6C7AF Soft Beige: This gentle, neutral tone complements the richer colors, adding a sense of balance and harmony to the overall palette. The soft beige represents the warmth, comfort, and inclusivity that Homecrate aims to provide to expats from all backgrounds, creating a welcoming and nurturing atmosphere.

  • Font Choices:

    Homecrate utilizes the Poppins font family, which is known for its friendly and approachable appearance. Poppins' rounded letterforms and open counters convey a sense of warmth and comfort, which aligns with Homecrate's brand identity as a company that provides expats with nostalgic items from their home countries. The various weights and styles of Poppins allow for flexibility in creating a visual hierarchy and emphasizing key information, making the content easily digestible and reinforcing the brand's commitment to clear communication.

  • User Interface Patterns:

      Grids: The design uses flex containers for structure, allowing for responsive layouts and easy organization of content. The use of grids enables a clean and consistent layout, which reflects the brand's professionalism and attention to detail.

      Dropdown Menu: The navigation features a dropdown menu, which help in organizing content into categories, enabling users to navigate the site efficiently. The organized menu structure makes it easy for users to navigate the site, reinforcing Homecrate's focus on providing a user-friendly experience that caters to the needs of expats seeking a taste of home.

      Carousel: The homepage features a carousel with three images, showcasing the company slogan ina dynamic way, and adds a dynamic visual element to the page.

      Video Container: The video container is responsive and positioned centrally, ensuring a consistent viewing experience across different devices. Video containers allow for the showcasing of customer testimonials and product unboxing experiences, which helps create an emotional connection between the brand and its target audience.
  • Structure of the Navigation and Content:

    The navigation is organized into a horizontal main menu, which provides easy browsing and access to content categories. While the desktop version does not have dropdown submenus, the mobile version features a submenu, ensuring a responsive and consistent user experience across different devices. The main content area uses flex containers, which enable organized content distribution and adaptability to various screen sizes. This organization allows for an intuitive and user-friendly browsing experience, tailored to the needs of Homecrate's expat customers who may be accessing the website from different devices and locations.

Lean Canvas

Fill in at least 2 bullet points per building block and according to the rubrics.

Problem

  • List your top 3 problems you solve for your target group.
    • 1. Diminishing / lack of connection to home
    • 2. Unavailibility of specific local items
    • 3. Inability to travel home frequently / difficulty with transporting specific itmes (limited space, liquids, perishables etc)

  • Describe briefly how the problems are solved now (existing alternatives).
    • 1. Telephonic contact, videochat, vacations home, reading local news
    • 2. Looking for alternatives, cooking/ making these items at home, bulk buying and taking on a palne
    • 3. Focus on work, wait

Solution

  • Outline the brand solution you have for the above problem(s).

    • Our solution is a subscription crate (homecrate) comprised of items, foodstuffs and goodies from any expats given country of origin. Though we cannot replace the human connection, we can lessen the sense of diminishing connection by sparking feelings of nostalgia and joy, as well as physically reminding our customers of their home countries through their senses. Flavours, smells, sights and touches can all evoke emotional responses, and the crates from Homecrate will all be filled with items specifically selected to envoke these responses, reegniting our custoemrs connection to their home.

    They will no longer need to look for suplements or alternatives, or make them at home. They will also not need to pack an extra suitcase to make space for these items.
