Production

Design Elements

Please provide a list of design elements alongside their justifications:

A colour scheme:
  • #541C1C Rich Maroon: This deep, warm hue exudes a sense of sophistication and stability, instilling confidence and trust in the brand. The rich maroon also creates a feeling of warmth and comfort, reflecting the nostalgic and homely essence of Homecrate's service.
  • #9F5B31 Warm Brown: This earthy, inviting color evokes feelings of familiarity and reliability, resonating with the brand's commitment to connecting expats with their roots. The warm brown also embodies the natural and authentic elements of the carefully curated packages, highlighting Homecrate's dedication to delivering genuine, heartfelt experiences.
  • #E6C7AF Soft Beige: This gentle, neutral tone complements the richer colors, adding a sense of balance and harmony to the overall palette. The soft beige represents the warmth, comfort, and inclusivity that Homecrate aims to provide to expats from all backgrounds, creating a welcoming and nurturing atmosphere.

Font Choices:

Homecrate uses the Poppins font family, which is known for its friendly and approachable appearance. We chose it because of its simplicity and minimalist look paired with a very circular, soft shape. This combination works well for us, since we want to appear contemporary, but also very warm, homely and friendly. The various weights and styles of Poppins allow for flexibility in creating a visual hierarchy and emphasizing key information, making the content easily digestible and reinforcing the brand's commitment to clear communication.

User Interface Patterns:

  • Sticky navigation bar: A horizontal navigation bar that provides users with easy access to different pages on the website. It sticks to the top of the screen, so users navigate the website from any part of the page. The navigation links include Home, Values, Visuals, Corporate and Contact us
  • Dropdown menu (phone only): The navigation bar is responsive and collapses into a dropdown menu when the website is viewed on mobile devices. This is implemented using the Bootstrap "navbar-toggler" class and a button that toggles the menu's visibility. This makes the nav bar not get in the way when viewed on a phone, and also allows the buttons that redirect to different pages to be bigger when the dropdown is engaged.
  • Links: The navigation links are used to navigate the website, directing users to the corresponding pages. These are created using the "nav-link" class.
  • Carousel: A carousel is used to showcase a series of images in a slideshow format. The carousel is created using the "carousel" class and related attributes to control its behavior, such as autoplay, looping, and navigation. Since the carousel is used to display our slogan, the navigation is disabled.
  • iframe (video box): An iframe is used to embed a YouTube video on the website. The video is set to autoplay, loop, and mute. The iframe is wrapped in a "video-container" div, which also includes an overlay with the brand name. The video makes the website feel more lively, engaging and dynamic.
  • Email input field: The email input field is used to submit the users email when signing up for our newsletter, or sending us a message. It will not allow the user to submit anything that is not in the format of an email adress
  • Textarea input field: The text input field is used to send us a message.
  • Button: Buttons are used for submitting a message for Homecrate, subscribing to the newsletter, and visiting our Facebook and Instagram pages.
  • Footer: The footer contains copyright information, social media icons, and links to the brand's social media pages. It is styled using the "sticky-bottom" class to ensure it stays at the bottom of the page.
  • Grids: The layout of the content is organized using Bootstrap's grid system, with "container-fluid," "row," and "col" classes to create a responsive and flexible layout.
  • Divs: The content is wrapped in div elements to group and style related elements. These divs are used in conjunction with the Bootstrap grid system to create a structured and organized layout.

Structure of the Navigation and Content:

The navigation is organized into a horizontal main menu, which provides easy browsing and access to content categories. While the desktop version does not have dropdown submenus, the mobile version features a submenu, ensuring a responsive and consistent user experience across different devices. The main content area uses flex containers, which enable organized content distribution and adaptability to various screen sizes. This organization allows for an intuitive and user-friendly browsing experience, tailored to the needs of Homecrate's expat customers who may be accessing the website from different devices and locations.

Testing Report

  • Your testing goals: what you are trying to learn about your website?
    • Since our website is a pre-launch website, we are mainly trying to learn how well visitors can navigate the basic layout and find out information about the brand. The website is quite simple in its structure, only having the one navigation which leads to the different parts of the page. As a result, in our testing, we tested how effectively visitors can reach and find different information about our company.
  • Your testing methods which includes information about:
    • The test’s participants(number, match with target audience, etc.) - 6 participants, most of them foreigners, though none specifically labeled “Expats”, as they were all students, and expats must be full time workers. It included both men and women, from the ages of 19 - 24.
    • The test’s setting (which material is used, is it done remotely, on campus, at home, etc.) - The test was conducted with simple verbal instructions, a new one given every time a task has been completed. It was done on campus, with a variety of devices.
    • The test’s protocol (what instructions are given, how it is recorded, etc.) - Instructions: [Find and read through our values - Then, go to the home page, and read about the founders of Homecrate - Then, find the font we use - Then, use our website to go directly to our Facebook profile - Lastly, sign up to our newsletter, or send us a message]. We recorded the screen and participants using a phone camera, always assigning a new task when the previous one was completed and intervening as little as possible.
  • Your testing results, which includes information about:
    • Positive and negative aspects of the UI/UX that have been identified, ranked by importance.
    • Positives: #1 Nobody was confused about navigating the actual site, #2 the menu was clear, and visitors were able to identify the different buttons to move around. #3 They were all able to find all the information in a relatively timely manner.
    • Negatives: #1 People were also trying to go back to the home page by clicking the homecrate logo, which only took them to the top of the current page. #2 Some people got confused when asked to find the information about the founders, for which they had to scroll down the home page, a lot of them went to the then present “contacts” tab. #3 Some also clicked on elements which were not clickable.
    • Improvements that have been implemented on the final website (or that would be implemented if doing so would be too complex) - “Contact” page was specified to “contact us”, and a space to leave a message and email was added. This was both to capture visitors and start building a base, but also to make it more clear what the point of the page is. Also, links to our Facebook and Instagram were added to the “Contact us” page; previously they were only in the footer, making them hard to find. Info from “Product” page was moved to home page, to reduce the number of clickable items. At the bottom of the home page, the option to subscribe to our newsletter was added, so that we can start to build a base of customers. we also made the homecrate logo in the top left corner take visitors back to the home page, instead of the top of the current page, becasue we observed that that was the way people tried to use it most of the time.

Marketing

Context of campaign and promotional activities

Explain the context of the campaign is (What the campaign is about, what the message is, which promotional activities were executed). In addition, present the objectives as mentioned in the Communication & Media Plan. Explain and justify the chosen channels/platforms used and show there is a solid connection to the objectives established in the Communication & Media Plan.

  • The Homecrate marketing campaign is designed to connect with the expat community by evoking feelings of nostalgia, promoting cultural pride, and fostering a sense of belonging among its audience. The campaign's primary message is to enable expats to cherish their cultural roots and create a strong bond with other expats who share similar experiences.
  • The marketing campaign includes a six-week content calendar that features a variety of promotional activities such as sharing trivia about different home countries, landscapes or landmarks, recipes, cultural traditions, and interactive posts that encourage community growth and engagement.
  • Our objectives were to Increase Instagram followers to 100 by the end of the six-week campaign, increase Facebook followers to 20 by the end of the six-week campaign, encourage audience engagement on both platforms, aiming for an average of 10 likes and 2 comments per post. Unfortunately, by the end of the six-week campaign, we only managed to gain 26 Instagram followers and 4 Facebook followers. Although we experienced some growth, it was quite a bit lower than our initial objectives.
  • The chosen channels for this campaign are Instagram and Facebook, which are popular platforms for sharing visual content and enabling easy interaction among users. These platforms align well with the campaign's objectives, as they provide opportunities for expats to engage with the content, share their stories, and connect with one another.

Learning Points

Identify and justify your learning point (based on the Communication & Media Plan). What did you learn during the duration of the project concerning the campaign? What worked and what didn't? What went as expected and what didn't? What would you do differently? Reflect on the past work and describe what you learned.

  • The main thing we learned was that marketing on social media is about a lot more than just posting. While many of the posts weren't bad, the lack of interaction and communication outside of them meant that our social media did not grow nearly as fast as we had hoped. There seemed to be a lack of momentum in the campaign, and after an initial influx, the numbers stagnated. If we were to do it again, we would focus more on active interaction and engagement, and trying to create and upkeep momentum, rather than just post mindlessly.

Problem

  • List your top 3 problems you solve for your target group.
    • 1. Diminishing / lack of connection to home
    • 2. Unavailability of specific local items
    • 3. Inability to travel home frequently / difficulty with transporting specific items (limited space, liquids, perishables etc)
  • Describe briefly how the problems are solved now (existing alternatives).
    • 1. Telephonic contact, video chat, vacations home, reading local news
    • 2. Looking for alternatives, cooking/making these items at home, bulk buying and taking on a plane
    • 3. Focus on work, simply having to wait

Solution

  • Outline the brand solution you have for the above problem(s).
  • Our solution is a subscription crate (homecrate) comprised of items, foodstuffs, and goodies from any expats given the country of origin. Though we cannot replace the human connection, we can lessen the sense of diminishing connection by sparking feelings of nostalgia and joy, as well as physically reminding our customers of their home countries through their senses. Flavors, smells, sights, and touches can all evoke emotional responses, and the crates from Homecrate will all be filled with items specifically selected to evoke these responses, reigniting our customers' connection to their home. They will no longer need to look for supplements or alternatives or make them at home. They will also not need to pack an extra suitcase to make space for these items.

Cost structure

  • List your main costs: define the fixed and variable costs, and calculate the cost per unit.

    • Variable Costs (per unit):
    • Products (snacks, goodies, cultural items): 17€ (after negotiating with suppliers)

    • Assuming a monthly subscription price of 59€ per box, the gross profit per unit would be 26.50€ (59€ - 32.50€).

    • Total cost per unit (including fixed and variable costs): 32.50€ (variable) + 16.86€ (fixed) = 49.36€

Validation of Assumptions

Write a reflection on the choices made in creating and choosing the trademark, including an analysis of the existing alternatives.

  • When creating and developing the Homecrate trademark, our intention was to develop a brand that resonated personally with the expat community. We picked it because we saw and understood the unique emotional needs and challenges faced by those living away from their home countries. This played a significant role in shaping the brand's identity, image, and unique value proposition.

  • The existing alternatives for tackling homesickness among expats largely focused on communication with friends and family, or maybe trying to recreate a taste of home by making local dishes from scratch. However, these do not fully address the emotional and sensory connection to one's home country. With Homecrate, we saw an opportunity to fill this gap by providing a thoughtfully curated subscription service that delivered a piece of home directly to expats.

  • The trademark "Homecrate" was chosen to evoke a sense of warmth and nostalgia while also communicating the core offering of the service—a crate filled with items reminiscent of home. The name is easily relatable and memorable, creating an instant connection with the target audience.

  • The brand's values, unique value proposition, and marketing materials all reflect a genuine understanding of the expat experience. They emphasize the importance of staying connected to one's roots and creating a sense of belonging through shared memories and emotions. The brand is built on a foundation of empathy, personal connection, and cultural understanding, which sets it apart from existing alternatives and creates a strong emotional bond with the target audience.
