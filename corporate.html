Design Elements

Please provide a list of design elements alongside their justifications:

  • A colour scheme

    • #541C1C Rich Maroon: This deep, warm hue exudes a sense of sophistication and stability, instilling confidence and trust in the brand. The rich maroon also creates a feeling of warmth and comfort, reflecting the nostalgic and homely essence of Homecrate's service.

    • #9F5B31 Warm Brown: This earthy, inviting color evokes feelings of familiarity and reliability, resonating with the brand's commitment to connecting expats with their roots. The warm brown also embodies the natural and authentic elements of the carefully curated packages, highlighting Homecrate's dedication to delivering genuine, heartfelt experiences.

    • #E6C7AF Soft Beige: This gentle, neutral tone complements the richer colors, adding a sense of balance and harmony to the overall palette. The soft beige represents the warmth, comfort, and inclusivity that Homecrate aims to provide to expats from all backgrounds, creating a welcoming and nurturing atmosphere.

  • Font Choices:

    Homecrate utilizes the Poppins font family, which is known for its friendly and approachable appearance. Poppins' rounded letterforms and open counters convey a sense of warmth and comfort, which aligns with Homecrate's brand identity as a company that provides expats with nostalgic items from their home countries. The various weights and styles of Poppins allow for flexibility in creating a visual hierarchy and emphasizing key information, making the content easily digestible and reinforcing the brand's commitment to clear communication.

  • User Interface Patterns:

      Navigation bar: A horizontal navigation bar that provides users with easy access to different pages on the website. The navigation links include Home, Values, Visuals, Corporate, Product, and Contact.

      Dropdown menu (phone only): The navigation bar is responsive and collapses into a dropdown menu when the website is viewed on mobile devices. This is implemented using the Bootstrap "navbar-toggler" class and a button that toggles the menu's visibility.

      Links: The navigation links are used to navigate the website, directing users to the corresponding pages. These are created using the "nav-link" class.

      Carousel: A carousel is used to showcase a series of images in a slideshow format. The carousel is created using the "carousel" class and related attributes to control its behavior, such as autoplay, looping, and navigation controls.

      iframe (video box): An iframe is used to embed a YouTube video on the website. The video is set to autoplay, loop, and mute, with controls hidden. The iframe is wrapped in a "video-container" div, which also includes an overlay with the brand name.

      Text box: Text boxes are used for submitting an email adress when signing up for the Homecrate newsletter, as well as sending us a custom message

      Button: Buttons are used for submitting a message for Homecrate, subscribing to the newsletter, and visiting our Facebook and Instagram pages

      Footer: The footer contains copyright information, social media icons, and links to the brand's social media pages. It is styled using the "sticky-bottom" class to ensure it stays at the bottom of the page.

      Grids: The layout of the content is organized using Bootstrap's grid system, with "container-fluid," "row," and "col" classes to create a responsive and flexible layout.

      Divs: The content is wrapped in div elements to group and style related elements. These divs are used in conjunction with the Bootstrap grid system to create a structured and organized layout.
  • Structure of the Navigation and Content:

    The navigation is organized into a horizontal main menu, which provides easy browsing and access to content categories. While the desktop version does not have dropdown submenus, the mobile version features a submenu, ensuring a responsive and consistent user experience across different devices. The main content area uses flex containers, which enable organized content distribution and adaptability to various screen sizes. This organization allows for an intuitive and user-friendly browsing experience, tailored to the needs of Homecrate's expat customers who may be accessing the website from different devices and locations.

Website Purpose

The purpose of this website - pre-launch website for our brand.

    There are a few reasons why it makes sense for Homecrate to have a pre-launch website:
    Our pre-launch website is a great way for us to spark interest in Homecrate before the big day arrives. By giving future customers a sneak peek at what we have to offer, we're igniting their curiosity and creating a buzz around our brand, setting the stage for a successful launch.
    Additionally, the website helps us understand our audience better. We can see what catches their attention, gather their feedback, and use this valuable information to fine-tune our offerings and marketing strategies. It's like having a conversation with our potential customers, making sure we're on the right track.
    Lastly, the website is our playground for perfecting our message and brand positioning. By observing how visitors interact with our content and listening to their input, we can craft a communication strategy that truly resonates with our target audience, ensuring that our voice is both clear and compelling.

Limitations

Technical and circumstancial limitations asociated with development of the website

  • Video Player Loading - When the video in the iframe loads, it's possible to see the title of the video, as well as the navigation options. Unfortunately I found no way of hiding this, other than cropping the video, which presented a separate set of issues.
  • Video Player not autoplaying on mobile - On mobile, the video in the iframe does not autoplay; this is because of limitations imposed by youtube, and since there is a div covering the video (to prevent interaction) it is not possible to play it at all. This could potentially be fixed by making a javascript which would click on the video, and afterwards make it non-interactible; however, this task was above my abilities.
  • We could not find people availible for website testing specifically in our nieche (Expats) so we asked our classmates and frineds, a lot of whom, while not expacts by definition (because students do not officially count as expats), do come from different countries, making their experience similar in many ways.
  • Our "Newsletter" and "Message us" options actually lead nowhere; this is because we are not actually gathering any data or emails. They are just a proof of concept.
  • Also, the "Newsletter" and "Message us" options were very late additions, and therfore, were not part of the first couple of testing. Had we had them earlier, we would have included them; however, as it is, only the later interviews now have this question.

Testing Report

  • Your testing goals: what you are trying to learn about your website?
    • Since our website is a pre-launch website, we are mainly trying to learn how well visitors can navigate the basic layout and find out information about the brand. The website is quite simple in its structure, only having the one navigation which leads to the different parts of the page. As a result, in our testing, we tested how effectively visitors can reach and find different information about our company.
  • Your testing methods which includes information about:
    • The test’s participants(number, match with target audience, etc.) - 6 participants, most of them foreigners, though none specifically labeled “Expats”, as they were all students, and expats must be full time workers. It included both men and women, from the ages of 19 - 24.
    • The test’s setting (which material is used, is it done remotely, on campus, at home, etc.) - The test was conducted with simple verbal instructions, a new one given every time a task has been completed. It was done on campus, with a variety of devices.
    • The test’s protocol (what instructions are given, how it is recorded, etc.) - Instructions: [Find and read through our values - Then, go to the home page, and read about the founders of Homecrate - Then, find the font we use - Then, use our website to go directly to our Facebook profile - Lastly, sign up to our newsletter, or send us a message]. We recorded the screen and participants using a phone camera, always assigning a new task when the previous one was completed and intervening as little as possible.
  • Your testing results, which includes information about:
    • Positive and negative aspects of the UI/UX that have been identified, ranked by importance.
      • Positives: Nobody was confused about navigating the actual site, the menu was clear, and visitors were able to identify the different buttons to move around. They were all able to find all the information in a relatively timely manner.
      • Negatives: Some people got confused when asked to find the information about the founders, for which they had to scroll down the home page. A lot of them went to the then present “contacts” tab. Some also clicked on elements which were not clickable.
    • Improvements that have been implemented on the final website (or that would be implemented if doing so would be too complex) - “Contact” page was specified to “contact us”, and a space to leave a message and email was added. This was both to capture visitors and start building a base, but also to make it more clear what the point of the page is. Also, links to our Facebook and Instagram were added to the “Contact us” page; previously they were only in the footer, making them hard to find. Info from “Product” page was moved to home page, to reduce the number of clickable items. At the bottom of the home page, the option to subscribe to our newsletter was added, so that we can start to build a base of customers.

Lean Canvas

Fill in at least 2 bullet points per building block and according to the rubrics.

Problem

  • List your top 3 problems you solve for your target group.
    • 1. Diminishing / lack of connection to home
    • 2. Unavailibility of specific local items
    • 3. Inability to travel home frequently / difficulty with transporting specific itmes (limited space, liquids, perishables etc)

  • Describe briefly how the problems are solved now (existing alternatives).
    • 1. Telephonic contact, videochat, vacations home, reading local news
    • 2. Looking for alternatives, cooking/ making these items at home, bulk buying and taking on a palne
    • 3. Focus on work, wait

Solution

  • Outline the brand solution you have for the above problem(s).

    • Our solution is a subscription crate (homecrate) comprised of items, foodstuffs and goodies from any expats given country of origin. Though we cannot replace the human connection, we can lessen the sense of diminishing connection by sparking feelings of nostalgia and joy, as well as physically reminding our customers of their home countries through their senses. Flavours, smells, sights and touches can all evoke emotional responses, and the crates from Homecrate will all be filled with items specifically selected to envoke these responses, reegniting our custoemrs connection to their home.

    They will no longer need to look for suplements or alternatives, or make them at home. They will also not need to pack an extra suitcase to make space for these items.
