Production

Design Elements

Please provide a list of design elements alongside their justifications:

A colour scheme:
  • #541C1C Rich Maroon: This deep, warm hue exudes a sense of sophistication and stability, instilling confidence and trust in the brand. The rich maroon also creates a feeling of warmth and comfort, reflecting the nostalgic and homely essence of Homecrate's service.
  • #9F5B31 Warm Brown: This earthy, inviting color evokes feelings of familiarity and reliability, resonating with the brand's commitment to connecting expats with their roots. The warm brown also embodies the natural and authentic elements of the carefully curated packages, highlighting Homecrate's dedication to delivering genuine, heartfelt experiences.
  • #E6C7AF Soft Beige: This gentle, neutral tone complements the richer colors, adding a sense of balance and harmony to the overall palette. The soft beige represents the warmth, comfort, and inclusivity that Homecrate aims to provide to expats from all backgrounds, creating a welcoming and nurturing atmosphere.

Font Choices:

Homecrate uses the Poppins font family, which is known for its friendly and approachable appearance. We chose it because of its simplicity and minimalist look paired with a very circular, soft shape. This combination works well for us, since we want to appear contemporary, but also very warm, homely and friendly. The various weights and styles of Poppins allow for flexibility in creating a visual hierarchy and emphasizing key information, making the content easily digestible and reinforcing the brand's commitment to clear communication.

User Interface Patterns:

  • Sticky navigation bar: A horizontal navigation bar that provides users with easy access to different pages on the website. It sticks to the top of the screen, so users navigate the website from any part of the page. The navigation links include Home, Values, Visuals, Corporate and Contact us
  • Dropdown menu (phone only): The navigation bar is responsive and collapses into a dropdown menu when the website is viewed on mobile devices. This is implemented using the Bootstrap "navbar-toggler" class and a button that toggles the menu's visibility. This makes the nav bar not get in the way when viewed on a phone, and also allows the buttons that redirect to different pages to be bigger when the dropdown is engaged.
  • Links: The navigation links are used to navigate the website, directing users to the corresponding pages. These are created using the "nav-link" class.
  • Carousel: A carousel is used to showcase a series of images in a slideshow format. The carousel is created using the "carousel" class and related attributes to control its behavior, such as autoplay, looping, and navigation. Since the carousel is used to display our slogan, the navigation is disabled.
  • iframe (video box): An iframe is used to embed a YouTube video on the website. The video is set to autoplay, loop, and mute. The iframe is wrapped in a "video-container" div, which also includes an overlay with the brand name. The video makes the website feel more lively, engaging and dynamic.
  • Email input field: The email input field is used to submit the users email when signing up for our newsletter, or sending us a message. It will not allow the user to submit anything that is not in the format of an email adress
  • Textarea input field: The text input field is used to send us a message.
  • Button: Buttons are used for submitting a message for Homecrate, subscribing to the newsletter, and visiting our Facebook and Instagram pages.
  • Footer: The footer contains copyright information, social media icons, and links to the brand's social media pages. It is styled using the "sticky-bottom" class to ensure it stays at the bottom of the page.
  • Grids: The layout of the content is organized using Bootstrap's grid system, with "container-fluid," "row," and "col" classes to create a responsive and flexible layout.
  • Divs: The content is wrapped in div elements to group and style related elements. These divs are used in conjunction with the Bootstrap grid system to create a structured and organized layout.

Structure of the Navigation and Content:

The navigation is organized into a horizontal main menu, which provides easy browsing and access to content categories. While the desktop version does not have dropdown submenus, the mobile version features a submenu, ensuring a responsive and consistent user experience across different devices. The main content area uses flex containers, which enable organized content distribution and adaptability to various screen sizes. This organization allows for an intuitive and user-friendly browsing experience, tailored to the needs of Homecrate's expat customers who may be accessing the website from different devices and locations.

Info from “Product” page was moved to home page, to reduce the number of clickable items. At the bottom of the home page, the option to subscribe to our newsletter was added, so that we can start to build a base of customers. we also made the homecrate logo in the top left corner take visitors back to the home page, instead of the top of the current page, becasue we observed that that was the way people tried to use it most of the time.

Marketing

Context of campaign and promotional activities

Homecrate's recently executed promotional campaign centered around our primary objective: expanding our core, loyal audience of expats in the Netherlands aged 18-35. We aimed to foster a deeper sense of community and connection by creating an engaging space for our audience to reminisce and converse about their home countries.

On Instagram, we deployed a content strategy primarily focusing on evocative visual media - we shared images and videos designed to stir up nostalgia, evoke memories, and spark conversations about their homeland. The intention was to evoke feelings of nostalgia and connection to home among our audience. We aimed to spur engagement by inviting them to share their memories related to the topics featured and hoped that this user-generated content would stimulate further engagement and reach.

Over on Facebook, we used the platform's community-centric strength to create a space for practical advice and shared experiences. Our posts, though informative and featuring images related to life abroad, were carefully crafted to incite dialogue about the challenges and joys of living abroad, as well as celebrating cultures around the world.

Linking the campaign back to our objectives, we hoped to stir conversations, build relationships, and stimulate curiosity about Homecrate, ultimately leading to increased website traffic and conversions.

However, the campaign didn't perform as well as we hoped. Engagement rates were lower than expected, and the resulting traffic to our website didn't meet our targets. While our campaign was rooted in a strong understanding of our target audience and leveraged the unique strengths of our chosen platforms, the results indicate a need for us to revisit and refine our approach.

Learning Points

Based on the outcome of our campaign and your observations, there are several key learning points that can help shape our future strategies:

  • Engagement is Key: While our content on Instagram and Facebook was crafted to incite conversation and evoke nostalgia, the lower-than-expected interaction underscores the need for more proactive engagement. In the future, we could take the initiative to spark discussions, ask questions in the comments under our posts, or create interactive content like polls or contests.
  • Community Building Efforts: We discovered that merely having a presence on Facebook doesn't automatically translate to community building. In hindsight, leveraging Facebook groups could have provided a more interactive platform for our audience, creating a space for them to share experiences and naturally integrate Homecrate into their discussions. Moving forward, we should look into setting up a dedicated group for our audience, and engaging in Facebook groups relevant to our target audience.
  • Active Participation on Instagram: Our campaign highlighted the importance of not just posting content, but also actively engaging with other accounts and our followers on Instagram. This could involve responding to comments on our posts, engaging with posts from our followers, or even collaborating with other expat-focused accounts.
  • Growth Strategies: While our focus was on building a core, loyal audience, the campaign revealed that we lacked strategies for audience growth. This might include tactics like Instagram promotions, influencer partnerships, or Facebook advertising. In the future, striking a balance between fostering a loyal community and pursuing growth will be important.
  • Consistent Activity: The campaign's results indicate that maintaining a consistent and active presence on both platforms is crucial. This doesn't just mean regular posting, but also consistent engagement with our followers and with relevant content from other accounts.

All in all, the learning points from this campaign revolve around increased engagement, community building, active participation, audience growth, and consistency. These insights will be invaluable in refining our approach and achieving more successful outcomes in our future campaigns.

Future Planning

  • Rethinking Engagement: In future campaigns, we would aim to stimulate proactive discussions rather than passively expecting them to occur. Implementing features like Instagram Stories Q&A sessions, Facebook Live discussions, or themed days (like a "Memory Monday" featuring stories from home countries) could promote active engagement.
  • Community Building via Facebook Groups: A notable shift would be establishing a Facebook group that functions as a support system for the expat community. This could provide a platform for open discussion, shared experiences, and it could seamlessly introduce Homecrate into these daily interactions. We would also become part of other groups related to expats.
  • Instagram Community Outreach: Rather than merely populating our Instagram feed, we would actively reach out to similar accounts, influencers, and even our audience's posts. Regular engagement such as this could stimulate reciprocal interactions, creating a sense of community.
  • Strategic Audience Expansion: Although nurturing a loyal audience would remain our primary goal, we would consider incorporating strategies aimed at growth. These could involve cross-promotions with other relevant accounts, paid promotions targeting our desired demographic, or even collaborations with expat influencers.
  • Consistency and Variation in Activity: Future campaigns would benefit from a steady presence across platforms, ensuring regular posting and interaction. However, we would look into adding variety to our content, including different formats and themes, to keep the audience engaged and curious.
  • Evolve Content Strategy: Our evocative content approach would continue but with a twist. We might experiment with behind-the-scenes sneak peeks into Homecrate's curating process, or perhaps user testimonials, which could bring an intriguing element to our narrative.

Problem

  • List your top 3 problems you solve for your target group.
    • 1. Diminishing / lack of connection to home
    • 2. Unavailability of specific local items
    • 3. Inability to travel home frequently / difficulty with transporting specific items (limited space, liquids, perishables etc)
  • Describe briefly how the problems are solved now (existing alternatives).
    • 1. Telephonic contact, video chat, vacations home, reading local news
    • 2. Looking for alternatives, cooking/making these items at home, bulk buying and taking on a plane
    • 3. Focus on work, simply having to wait

Solution

  • Outline the brand solution you have for the above problem(s).
  • Our solution is a subscription crate (homecrate) comprised of items, foodstuffs, and goodies from any expats given the country of origin. Though we cannot replace the human connection, we can lessen the sense of diminishing connection by sparking feelings of nostalgia and joy, as well as physically reminding our customers of their home countries through their senses. Flavors, smells, sights, and touches can all evoke emotional responses, and the crates from Homecrate will all be filled with items specifically selected to evoke these responses, reigniting our customers' connection to their home. They will no longer need to look for supplements or alternatives or make them at home. They will also not need to pack an extra suitcase to make space for these items.
